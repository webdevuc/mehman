Pegasus Airline

Pegasus Airline flies domestically and internationally (Kurtkoy region). Additionally, it has a counter/base at several Turkish airports. In 1989, Net, Silkar, and Aer Lingus joined together to form Pegasus Airlines, officially founded in 1990. After its formation in 1990, Pegasus Airlines immediately started operating flights (April). A war between Iraq and Kuwait broke out four months later, affecting travel to Turkey. Tourists began returning to Turkey in 1992, and Pegasus Airlines began to expand. As a result, Aer Lingus and Net withdrew their investments in 1994. As a result, Pegasus Airlines became a 100 percent Turkish-owned corporation in 1994. A top-rated international airline, Pegasus Airlines has developed steadily since then. Pegasus Airlines currently has a fleet of 92 passenger planes. The massive size of its fleet allows it to serve over one hundred and ten different cities worldwide. ESAS Holding is Pegasus Airlines' parent company. After purchasing Pegasus Airlines in 2005, ESAS Holding transformed the airline into a low-cost carrier. Cheaper Pegasus Airlines fares and improved onboard service were a consequence of this. Current ESAS Holding Chairman Ali Sabanci is a Turkish national.

History

Pegasus is Turkey's leading low-cost airline, offering point-to-point service on short and medium-range flight routes at affordable prices and aiming to build an extensive flight network with frequent service for travelers.

Pegasus Airlines, Turkey's second-largest airline, inked a contract in 2012 for up to 100 A320neo Family aircraft (57 A320neo and 18 A321neo types), of which 75 were definite orders. It became the first Turkish airline to purchase the A320neo from Airbus. When this directive was made public on December 18, 2012, it was in honor of Turkish Transport Minister Binali Yldrm, who was present for the announcement. Pegasus Airlines purchased 49 percent of Kyrgyz airline Air Manas in June 2012. On March 22, 2013, Pegasus Asia flew its maiden commercial trip under the company's new name.

A joint venture between Aer Lingus Group, Silkar Yatrm ve Insaat Organizasyonu A.S., and Net Holding A.S., Pegasus Hava Tasimaciligi A.S. began commercial operations in 1990 with two aircraft. Following its acquisition by Sevket Sabanci and his family's Esas Holding A.S., Pegasus began domestic flights in November 2005 and became the fourth-highest ranked scheduled carrier in Turkey.

After the IPO, 34.5 percent of the shares are traded on the Istanbul Stock Exchange, while Esas Holding A.S. holds 65.5 percent of the shares, and Sevket Sabanci and his family own the balance.
