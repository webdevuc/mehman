History

Thailand's flag airline, Thai Airways International Public Company Limited, is a Thai state-owned enterprise. Local, regional and transcontinental flights from Bangkok's Suvarnabhumi Airport go to major international and domestic destinations. As of 9 May 2005, the Thai government owns 53.77 percent of the company's fully paid up share capital, totaling 16,988,765,500 Baht. Total assets of the firm were 193,211 million Baht at the end of September 2004. For the last 40 years, THAI has been profitable in all of its businesses

It was a joint venture between Thai Airways Company (TAC) and Scandinavian Airlines System (SAS) that saw the establishment of Thai Airways International in 1960, with SAS giving an initial 30% share capital of two million Baht. Furthermore, SAS contributed operational, administrative, and marketing know-how, as well as training help, with the goal of quickly establishing a completely autonomous national airline. In 1987, the percentage of expatriate personnel in Thailand had dropped to less than one percent, after years of training and experience allowed Thai natives to take full management responsibilities.
