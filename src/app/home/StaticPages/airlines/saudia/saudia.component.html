History

In 1945, President Franklin D. Roosevelt presented King Abdul Aziz Ibn Saud with a Douglas DC-3 propeller-driven aircraft. This historic occasion highlighted the Kingdom's steady rise in civil aviation and the establishment of Saudi Airlines.

The airline's initial focus was on transporting Hajj pilgrims from other cities in Saudi Arabia to Jeddah. After a few decades, the Douglas DC-3s were replaced by newer aircraft types, and Saudi quickly began flying passengers to various destinations all over the world.
