SriLankan Airline

SriLankan Airline is the flag airline of Sri Lanka and was founded in 1947 at Air Ceylon. It is based at Bandaranaike International Airport, where it has a hub (CMB ). Over 95 destinations in 50 countries spanning Asia, the Middle East, Oceania, and Europe are served by SriLankan Airlines. There are codeshare agreements between the carrier, six Oneworld members, and eleven non-Alliance carriers. Six Airbus A320-200 planes, two Airbus A321-200 planes, seven Airbus A330-200 planes, four Airbus A330-300 planes, and one Airbus A340-300 plane make up the airline's fleet of 20 aircraft. Business Class and Economy Class are available on each flight.

Origin and Brief History

The primary airline in Sri Lanka is Srilankan Airlines. After the shutdown of Air Ceylon in 1979, the government established Air Lanka. Air Lanka began flying to 26 different locations in 1990. The Dubai-based Emirates Group bought it out in 1998. A new image and a new fleet were created when Emirates purchased 43.6 percent of Air Lanka's stock. The Srilankan Airlines name was introduced.

At the end of July 2001, a terrorist assault on Bandaranaike airport grounded half of Airlines' aircraft. The Airlines continued to grow in the years after this disaster, becoming the biggest Indian-bound airline. Up to 100 flights, a week are provided to 11 destinations. The company introduced its frequent flier program, FlySmiles, in 2008 after splitting with Emirates and benefiting from the latter's frequent flyer program.

A unique award was given to Srilankan Airlines in South Asia because of its dedication and progress in 2013. The Airlines acquired Mihin Lanka's operations in 2016 as part of an expansion strategy. As the Airlines has grown and improved its services, it has gained the respect of its consumers. Airlines got the First Star in the International Airline category at the Golden City Gate Awards at the ITB Berlin in 2020 to recognize its outstanding service quality. A new long-haul route to Melbourne, Australia, was inaugurated by Sri Lankan in October 2017, marking the airline's most ambitious expansion to date and its first new long-haul route in over five years. A direct plane connection between Australia and Sri Lanka has been restored after a 16-year absence.

Since the outbreak of the COVID-19 epidemic, the Airlines has been transporting goods and providing relief flights for those affected. There was a relief flight from Wuhan, China, on February 1, 2020. Until August 2020, the Airline had lost 36.3 billion rupees, and the government had granted a 1.46 billion voluntary retirement payout for 560 workers.
